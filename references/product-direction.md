# Sambook Product Direction

## One-Line Definition

Sambook is a Korean-first writing playground where people write short stories from three random words and read how others imagined from the same words.

## Core Premise

The product is not a broad social network. It is a repeatable writing game.

Every session begins with three words. The words should feel specific enough to spark a scene, but open enough to let different writers go in completely different directions.

Example:

- `고래`
- `엘리베이터`
- `유언장`

The fun comes from comparison:

- I wrote this from these three words.
- Someone else used the same words very differently.
- Today's common prompt becomes a small shared stage.

## Product Pillars

### Three-Word Prompt

The prompt is always three Korean words.

Each word should eventually carry metadata:

- part of speech
- difficulty
- mood
- genre affinity
- concreteness
- safety level

The early product can start with a static seed list, but the data model should assume a large curated word bank.

### Short Writing

Sambook should make writing feel light.

The default writing unit is short:

- 300 characters for a quick piece
- 800 characters for a normal piece
- 1500 characters for a longer piece

Titles are optional. The three words should be visible while writing.

At first, the app should not hard-block publishing if the words are not used. It can show usage status and encourage inclusion.

### Public and Private Memory

A story can be public or private.

- Public stories appear in public feeds and same-prompt pages.
- Private stories stay in the writer's own archive.

This lets the product work even before there is a large community.

### Same Words, Different Stories

The strongest browsing experience is not a generic feed. It is a same-prompt collection.

When a user finishes a story, the most natural next action is to read other stories written from the same three words.

## Signature Feature

### Today's Three Words

Fully random prompts are good for private play, but they scatter attention.

Sambook should also provide one shared daily prompt:

> Today's three words: `비상구`, `감`, `첫눈`

This becomes the daily public square. Random mode remains the private writing path.

## Target Feeling

Sambook should feel like:

- a writing notebook
- a small literary game
- a quiet public shelf

It should not feel like:

- a productivity tool
- a noisy SNS feed
- an AI writing generator
- a generic blogging platform

## Initial Product Promise

Give me three words, and I will see what kind of story I can make from them.
